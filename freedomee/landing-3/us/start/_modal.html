What is a token, or crypto-asset?

Before understanding what is a token or crypto asset, it is necessary to understand what is cryptocurrency. While this term is largely used to describe any digital currency, there are many differences between them. The characteristics below will help you to understand what a token is:

  • They are registered in the public database of another cryptocurrency;
  • Any real-world asset, tangible or intangible, can be registered as a token;
  • They may or may not serve as a exchange currency;
  • They have a wide range of potential functions such as: exclusive discounts, access to systems and voting rights;
  • For example: a token that represents an amount of physical asset, can freely be trade among its users, therefore with no governments or private company interventions.

What are tokens used for?

A token is to represent an asset in digital form by using blockchain technology, a system that registers transactions. These registers transactions are public, permanent and incensurable.
